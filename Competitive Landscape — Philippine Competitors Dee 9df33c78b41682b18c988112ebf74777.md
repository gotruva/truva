# Competitive Landscape — Philippine Competitors Deep Dive

This page breaks down every Philippine player that overlaps with our product, what they actually do, and where they fall short. Knowing this cold is essential for any investor or co-founder pitch.

---

## How to Think About Competitors

There are three types of competition we face:

1. **Direct** — Products that are trying to do the same thing (compare savings rates, help Filipinos find better yield)
2. **Indirect / Content** — Blogs and media sites publishing manual rate lists (our biggest Day 1 competition for search traffic)
3. **Adjacent** — Investment and banking platforms with partial overlap but different core job

None of them — not a single one — does what we do. But each one is a reason a user might not come to us, so we need to know them well.

## Category 1: Direct Competitors

### 1. Lemoneyd ([lemoneyd.com](http://lemoneyd.com))

**Type:** Web-based savings rate comparison site + iOS/Android finance tracker app

**Founder:** Rainier Jonathan Bayaca (indie developer)

**Community:** 45K+ users, active Facebook Group

**What it does:** Two products under one brand:

- **Web ([lemoneyd.com/savings](http://lemoneyd.com/savings)):** A clean comparison table of PH digital bank savings rates — Maya, Tonik, BPI BanKo, RCBC DiskarTech, MariBank, OwnBank, Netbank, GoTyme, CIMB, UNO, UnionDigital. Filterable by features (Daily Interest, Cashback, Free Transfers, etc.). Also covers Credit Cards, Invest & Trade, Digital Insurance.
- **Mobile app (iOS + Android):** Personal finance / expense tracker with budget tracking, investment monitoring (PH/US stocks, MP2, mutual funds), and insurance reminders. Freemium subscription model.

**Business model:** Affiliate referral codes on every rate card (DragonFi, MariBank, GoTyme, Maya, Netbank). App subscription revenue. Ad-free.

**Strengths:**

- Most product-like direct competitor — an actual comparison table, not a blog post
- Clean consumer-friendly UI with feature filters
- Active community (45K+ users, Facebook Group)
- iOS + Android app with real distribution
- Affiliate referral codes already embedded across multiple banks

**Critical weaknesses vs us:**

- Gross rates only — no after-tax computation (20% withholding tax never applied)
- No calculator — cannot compute actual peso earnings
- No Pag-IBIG MP2 on savings page
- No RTBs or UITFs
- No DeFi / stablecoin yields
- No PDIC Smart Split optimizer
- No time deposit tracker or maturity reminders
- Rate data is monthly, not daily (last updated Feb 1, 2026)
- No personalized calculator for specific amount + time horizon
- Mobile app and website are separate products with different jobs

**Our edge:** Lemoneyd shows you *what rates exist.* We show you *what you actually take home after tax, on your specific amount, over your specific time horizon, versus every option including DeFi.* They are a rate directory. We are an optimizer.

**Threat level: HIGH.** They own search real estate for PH digital bank comparison, have an active user community, and their UI is closest to ours. They could add a calculator relatively quickly.

**Strategic response:** Launch with the after-tax calculator as the hero feature above the fold — immediately differentiate from Lemoneyd's static table.

### 2. Finnest App (FinnestPH)

**Type:** Mobile app (Android)

**What it does:** Personal budget tracker that simulates how PH digital bank interest accumulates over time. Users manually input balances and the app auto-credits simulated daily/monthly interest for Maya, SeaBank, GoTyme, CIMB, Tonik, UnionDigital and others. Also tracks subscriptions, expenses, and credit cards.

**Business model:** Appears free / freemium

**Strengths:**

- First PH app to simulate digital bank interest accumulation
- Privacy-first (no bank logins, no sensitive data)
- Good Play Store ratings
- Covers multiple PH digital banks

**Critical weaknesses vs us:**

- Simulates interest only — does NOT compare rates or help you find the best one
- No Pag-IBIG MP2 comparison
- No RTB comparison
- No UITF comparison
- No DeFi / stablecoin yields
- No PDIC optimization feature
- No time deposit maturity reminders
- No calculator to show after-tax, after-FX take-home
- Android only — no web, no iOS
- Passive tracker, not an optimizer

**Our edge:** Finnest tells you how much your money is growing *where it already is.* We tell you where it *should be* to grow the most. Completely different jobs.

### 2. [Moneymax.ph](http://Moneymax.ph)

**Type:** Web-based financial product comparison site

**Parent:** MoneyHero Group (NASDAQ: MNY) — publicly listed Singapore-based company

**SEC registration:** Moneyguru Philippines Corporation

**What it does:** Compares car insurance, motorcycle insurance, travel insurance, credit cards, and personal loans in the Philippines. Has a savings account comparison section, but it is clearly secondary to their core business — credit cards and insurance are their primary affiliate revenue drivers. Licensed by the Insurance Commission as a financial and insurance broker. Over 40 million Filipinos served since 2014; NASDAQ-listed parent (MoneyHero Group, ticker MNY).

**Business model:** Affiliate commissions from financial product signups

**Strengths:**

- Established brand, significant SEO authority
- Publicly listed parent company with resources
- Licensed by the Insurance Commission
- Covers loans and credit cards comprehensively
- Has a savings account comparison section

**Critical weaknesses vs us:**

- Savings account comparison is static, not real-time
- No yield calculator — just a table
- No after-tax computation
- No DeFi / stablecoin comparison
- No Pag-IBIG MP2 comparison
- No PDIC Smart Split optimizer
- No time deposit tracker or reminders
- Focused on *loans and credit cards* as primary product — savings is secondary
- Not designed for the "where do I put my savings to earn the most" use case
- Singaporean parent company means less cultural alignment with Filipino savers

**Our edge:** Moneymax is a lending comparison tool that also has a savings tab. We are a savings optimization tool. Very different user intent.

**Important note:** Moneymax is the closest thing to a direct threat *at scale* because of their SEO muscle and affiliate model. If they ever build a proper savings calculator and add DeFi + MP2, they could challenge us. But they haven’t in years of operating — it’s clearly not their priority. Their revenue is driven by credit cards and insurance, not savings.

**Phase 2 note (Month 6+):** When Truva expands into credit card comparison, Moneymax becomes a direct competitor in that vertical. They have a 12-year head start in credit cards. Our edge will be a more engaged, savings-first audience — users who already trust Truva’s math — plus deeper after-tax and yield framing on card rewards (e.g. “what does 5% cashback actually earn you vs keeping cash in Maya?”). Moneymax shows rewards. Truva will show real value.

## Category 2: Content / Indirect Competitors

These are media sites and blogs that publish rate comparison *articles* — not tools. They are our biggest SEO competition at launch, not product competition. Over time, a good product beats a blog post.

### 3. BitPinas

**Type:** Philippine crypto and fintech media site

**What it does:** Publishes a monthly updated article titled "List of PH Digital Banks with High Interest Rates" — manually updated, with a table of current rates across digital banks. Very popular with crypto-aware Filipinos.

**Strengths:**

- Strong SEO — ranks highly for digital bank rate searches
- Regular updates (monthly)
- Trusted brand in PH crypto/fintech community
- Covers both digital banks and crypto context

**Critical weaknesses vs us:**

- A blog post, not a product
- No calculator
- No after-tax computation
- No Pag-IBIG MP2 or RTB or UITF
- No DeFi yields
- No PDIC optimizer
- No TD reminders
- Updated monthly — not real-time
- Cannot personalize to a user's specific amount, risk tolerance, or time horizon

**Our edge:** We are the product version of their article. When a user finds our calculator, they won't go back to reading a table in a blog post.

## Category 3: Adjacent Competitors (Partial Overlap)

### 7. Investagrams

**Type:** Philippine stock and multi-market trading platform

**What it does:** Real-time PSE, US stocks, crypto, forex, commodities tracking with charting tools, community, paper trading, and 10,000+ hours of educational content.

**Overlap with us:** Minimal. Investagrams targets *traders* who are actively watching markets. Our user wants to *park savings safely and earn more* — a completely different behavior and risk appetite.

**Why we don't compete:** Different user, different product, different job. Investagrams is for people who want to grow money through market participation. We're for people who want to earn more on safe savings without market risk.

---

### 8. GInvest / GCash

**Type:** In-app investment feature within GCash

**What it does:** Lets GCash users invest in UITFs (money market, equity, bond funds) and access government bonds (RTBs via [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph)). Also has GStocks for PSE trading.

**Overlap with us:** GInvest covers UITFs and RTBs — two product categories we track. But GInvest is a *product* (you buy UITFs through it), not a *comparison tool* (you compare yields across providers).

**Why we don't compete:** GInvest is a closed ecosystem — it only shows GCash-partnered fund products. We show all funds, all banks, all DeFi protocols, and let the user pick. We're neutral. GInvest is a seller.

---

### 9. [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph)

**Type:** Government-backed RTB investment app

**Parent:** Bureau of Treasury + UnionBank + PDAX

**What it does:** Lets Filipinos buy Philippine Retail Treasury Bonds starting at ₱5,000 via smartphone.

**Overlap with us:** RTBs are one row in our comparison table. [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph) is the execution platform — users buy RTBs there. We are the comparison layer that tells users *whether* RTBs are their best option on a given day.

**Relationship potential:** [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph) is actually a **referral partner opportunity**, not a competitor. We show RTB rates → link to [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph) → earn affiliate fee.

## Expanded Coverage Gap

Beyond the competitors above, a product audit identified **eight additional capital-stable yield products** that no Philippine comparison tool currently tracks. These are documented in full in the Founder Brief sub-page: **Expanded Product Coverage — New Financial Products to Aggregate**.

Summary of newly identified gaps:

| Product | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| T-Bills (91/182/364-day) | Sovereign, short-duration — BTr data is public and weekly |
| FXTNs | 2–10 year government paper — long-horizon savers |
| Dollar Time Deposits | Critical for OFW segment; taxed at 7.5% vs 20% for peso TDs |
| Dollar RTBs / Global Bonds | USD sovereign paper — periodic BTr issuance |
| Cooperative deposits (6–12%) | Highest yields in capital-stable category; no aggregator covers this |
| Landbank / DBP / OFBank | Government-owned banks — trusted by conservative savers who won’t use Maya |
| SSS PESO Fund | Tax-exempt, government-backed, massively underutilized by members |
| GSIS savings programs | Government employee segment — teachers, civil servants |

The cooperative deposit gap in particular is a **strategic moat opportunity** — high effort to collect data, but near-impossible for any competitor to replicate once built.

## The Key Insight for Your Pitch

**There is no dedicated savings rate comparison and optimization tool in the Philippines.** 

Moneymax is the closest, but their savings section is an afterthought to their core loans and credit cards business — and they haven't added a calculator, MP2, DeFi, or PDIC optimizer in years of operating. Finnest is the most product-like, but it's a tracker not a comparator — it tells you how much your current savings are growing, not where to move them.

This means:

1. **The market is validated** — BitPinas and [FintechNews.ph](http://FintechNews.ph) get significant traffic for manually-updated rate articles. There is clear demand.
2. **The product gap is real** — not one of these players has built an interactive, personalized, after-tax savings optimizer.
3. **The moat window is open** — Moneymax *could* build this but hasn't in years. Finnest is focused on budgeting. BitPinas is a media company. Nobody has made the savings comparison tool their primary product.

## Global Comparables (For Investor Context)

If asked "what's the global equivalent?" — the answer is **NerdWallet** (US) and **MoneySuperMarket** (UK). Both are multi-billion dollar comparison businesses built on exactly this model: free rate comparison + affiliate revenue + B2B data. Neither operates in the Philippines. The Philippine market has the demand (20M digital bank users) but none of the product sophistication. That is the opportunity.

We have identified six international analogues with specific actionable lessons for Truva. Full breakdown in the dedicated research page: **International Comparable Analysis — Global Market Analogues** (see sub-pages). NerdWallet is the primary benchmark — full entry below.

---

### NerdWallet (USA) 🇺🇸 — Primary International Benchmark

**Founded:** 2009 | **Status:** NASDAQ-listed (NRDS) | **Scale:** 23M+ monthly users, $379.6M revenue (2021) | **Operates in:** USA, UK, Canada, Australia

**What they are:** The world's largest personal finance comparison platform. Started with $800 and a single credit card comparison page. Now covers 8 verticals — credit cards, banking, mortgages, investing, insurance, personal loans, SMB, student loans — on a 100% free product monetized through affiliate commissions.

**Their expansion arc (Truva's exact roadmap in miniature):**

- 2009: Credit cards only
- 2012–2014: Added banking and savings rates
- 2015–2018: Added mortgages, loans, investing
- 2019–2021: Added SMB, insurance; IPO on NASDAQ
- 2024: Added NerdWallet+ subscription ($49/yr) and mortgage brokerage

**Revenue model:** Affiliate commissions paid per approved account, per click, or per funded loan. Credit cards = highest CPA ($50–200 USD per approval). Banking/savings = lower CPA, high volume. This is why credit cards are Truva's planned Phase 2 — it's NerdWallet's single highest-revenue vertical.

**Why they don't compete with Truva:** No Philippines operations. No PH digital bank coverage, no Pag-IBIG MP2, no BTr data, no Philippine withholding tax treatment. The Philippine market is completely open.

**What Truva should copy from NerdWallet's mobile UX (Beto's direct observation):**

NerdWallet's mobile experience is the clearest reference for handling rate comparison on small screens. Key patterns:

1. **Cards replace tables on mobile.** Each product = a vertical card (rate headline → best-for label → 2–3 specs → single CTA). No horizontal table scrolling. Lemoneyd uses horizontal table scrolling on mobile — a known UX antipattern. Truva should launch mobile-first with cards.
2. **Pre-qual flow before results.** 2–3 questions first (amount, time horizon, risk) → personalized card list. Increases affiliate CTR significantly.
3. **Sticky filter tabs.** Category navigation (All / Banks / Govt / UITFs / DeFi) pinned to top on scroll.
4. **Progressive disclosure.** Cards show minimum info to decide whether to click. Full details behind an expand/modal.
5. **Table of contents on long pages.** Sticky TOC on mobile so users jump to sections without scrolling.
6. **Trust-first design system ("Currency").** Clean white backgrounds, strong typography, blue CTAs, generous white space. Feels institutional.

**Full mobile UX breakdown + 3 actionable design patterns (Actions 12–14):** See International Comparable Analysis — Section 6: NerdWallet.

### The Pattern That Wins in Every Market

All six companies followed the same arc: (1) Free comparison content as acquisition hook, (2) Calculator or personalization as upgrade trigger, (3) Affiliate commissions as primary revenue, (4) Email alerts as retention loop, (5) B2B data licensing as revenue ceiling-raiser. Truva's current plan already follows this arc.
