# Maya Savings Review (2026): Is it still the best digital bank?

<SEOBox title="The Direct Answer">
  Maya remains the **best overall digital bank** in the Philippines for balances up to ₱100,000, offering a base rate of 3.5% that can be boosted to **14% p.a.** through mission-based spending.

Maya (formerly PayMaya) has transformed from a simple wallet into a full-featured digital bank. In this review, we break down the rates, the "catch," and how to maximize your after-tax yield.

## Key Facts & Rates

| Feature | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Base Interest Rate** | 3.5% p.a. |
| **Max Possible Rate** | 14% p.a. (via missions) |
| **Balance Cap for High Rate** | ₱100,000 |
| **PDIC Insured?** | Yes, up to ₱500,000 |
| **Withdrawal Fee** | Free to Maya users, ₱15-18 for others |

## Why it wins for many Filipinos

Maya's mission system is its primary moat. While traditional banks like BDO or BPI might offer 0.0625%, Maya starts you at 3.5% just for keeping your money there.

### The Mission System Explained

To get the headline 14% rate, you need to perform "missions" such as:
- Paying with Maya QR
- Paying bills
- Buying load

For most users, hitting the 10% mark is relatively easy with normal monthly spending.

## What to watch out for

1. **The ₱100k Ceiling**: After ₱100,000, your interest rate drops back to the base rate. If you have ₱1M, check out our [Time Deposit comparisons](/banking/rates).
2. **Engagement Fatigue**: You have to "earn" your rate every month.

## Final Verdict

**Best for:** Active spenders who can hit missions and keep under ₱100k.
**Not for:** Passive savers who want to "set it and forget it" for large amounts.
