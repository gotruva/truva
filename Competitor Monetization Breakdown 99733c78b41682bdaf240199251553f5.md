## Detailed Breakdown

### 1. Lemoneyd — Affiliate Referral Codes + App Subscription

The most instructive competitor because they are running the **same affiliate model we plan to run, on the same product category.**

- **Primary: Affiliate referral codes** — every bank on the savings page has an embedded referral code (LEMONEYD for DragonFi, LEMONEYDPH for MariBank, GOLEMONEYD for GoTyme, MAYALEMONEYD for Maya). Displayed prominently in a rotating banner above the comparison table.
- **Secondary: Mobile app subscription** — the Lemoneyd Finance Tracker app has a freemium model. App Store reviews confirm it is ad-free, so subscription is the only in-app revenue stream.
- **No display ads** — website and app are ad-free.

**What this tells us:**

1. The affiliate model already works for PH savings comparison — direct validation that banks pay for savings account signups from comparison sites.
2. Users accept referral codes on comparison sites without friction.
3. The gap they leave open is the calculator — Lemoneyd earns from showing rates but leaves "how much do I actually earn?" unanswered. That is our hero feature.
4. We can earn more per user — a user who calculates ₱500K has far higher purchase intent than someone who browsed a rate table. Higher intent = higher affiliate conversion.

### 2. Finnest App — No Revenue Model

Free Android app with no visible paid tier, no affiliate links to banks, no ads. Appears to be a passion project or early-stage product that hasn't figured out monetization yet.

**What this tells us:** They've validated the market need (people want to track savings across PH digital banks) but haven't figured out how to monetize it. This is a signal, not a warning. The market is waiting for someone to come in and do it properly.

**Our advantage:** We launch with a clear three-stream revenue model from day one.

---

### 2. [Moneymax.ph](http://Moneymax.ph) — CPA Affiliate + Insurance Brokerage

Confirmed directly from their About page:

- **Primary: CPA affiliate commissions** — Moneymax receives a fee per closed deal from partner banks, lenders, and insurers. When a user applies for a credit card or loan through Moneymax and gets approved, the financial institution pays Moneymax. This is a Cost-Per-Acquisition (CPA) model.
- **Secondary: Insurance brokerage fees** — Their subsidiary MoneyHero Insurance Brokerage, Inc. is a licensed insurance broker (IC License No. IB-15-2022-R). They earn commission on every insurance policy sold through the platform.
- **Tertiary: Sponsored / featured placement** — Banks pay to appear prominently in comparison results. Always disclosed as "advertised."
- **Bonus: Co-marketing partnerships** — Paid partnerships with FWD Group, GCash, foodpanda, and ShopBack.

**Revenue scale:** Part of MoneyHero Group (NASDAQ: MNY), reaching 9.8 million monthly users across 4 markets (PH, SG, HK, TW). This is a real scaled business — proof that the CPA affiliate model works in PH at significant scale.

**What this tells us:** Our Stream 2 (affiliate referrals) follows the exact same playbook Moneymax has proven. The difference: our referrals are higher-trust users with higher purchase intent — someone actively comparing savings rates to optimize ₱500K is more committed than someone casually browsing credit cards.

### 3. BitPinas — Media / Ads / Sponsorships

- **Display advertising** — standard banner and programmatic ads across the site
- **Sponsored content** — financial institutions (digital banks, crypto exchanges) pay for featured articles
- **Affiliate links** — links to [Coins.ph](http://Coins.ph), PDAX, and digital bank signups likely carry referral codes
- **Newsletter sponsorships** — brands sponsor their weekly crypto/fintech newsletter
- **Event coverage sponsorships** — Philippine Blockchain Week and similar events

**Revenue model type:** Media business. Revenue tied to pageviews and sponsorship deals — not recurring or scalable like SaaS.

**What this tells us:** BitPinas gets meaningful traffic from their monthly rate list article. That traffic is our target audience. When we show up with a better product (interactive calculator vs. static table), users will switch. Their SEO strength is temporary leverage for us — we publish content, appear in the same searches, and convert them with the product.

### 4. [FintechNews.ph](http://FintechNews.ph) — Sponsored Content + Display Ads

- **Sponsored articles / content marketing** — fintech companies pay for editorial coverage and "partner content" labels
- **Display advertising** — Google AdSense + direct ad sales
- **Newsletter / email list** — financial brands sponsor email sends to their subscriber base
- **Event partnerships** — sponsors fintech industry events in PH

**Revenue model type:** Niche fintech media. High-quality audience (fintech decision-makers, developers, investors) but lower volume than mass-market sites. Revenue is respectable but not large.

### 5. [Grit.ph](http://Grit.ph) — Affiliate + Ads + Courses

- **Primary: Affiliate commissions** — [Grit.ph](http://Grit.ph) carries affiliate links throughout their articles to digital banks, stockbrokers, insurance providers, and investment platforms. Every "best digital bank" article has tracked links.
- **Display advertising** — banner ads
- **Sponsored content** — financial brands pay for featured articles
- **Digital products** — personal finance guides, courses, and tools sold directly to readers

**Revenue model type:** Classic finance blog monetization. Earns meaningfully because their articles have high purchase intent — someone reading "best savings account" is likely to click and sign up.

**What this tells us:** [Grit.ph](http://Grit.ph) has proven that PH personal finance content converts to affiliate revenue. They're not just publishing for traffic — they're earning from it. This validates our affiliate stream.

### 6. [JuanInvestor.com](http://JuanInvestor.com) — Affiliate + Ads

- **Affiliate commissions** — primary revenue, links to investment platforms (COL Financial, FirstMetroSec), digital banks, and crypto platforms
- **Display advertising** — standard banners
- **Sponsored reviews** — financial products pay for review articles

**Scale:** Smaller than [Grit.ph](http://Grit.ph). Single operator or very small team. Moderate affiliate earnings.

---

### 7. Investagrams — Subscription + Broker Referrals

Most important competitor to study because they are the **only PH finance product with a working subscription model.**

- **Primary: Premium subscription (InvestaOne)** — paid tier unlocking advanced charting, real-time signals, trading journal analytics, and performance statistics. Priced at PH market rates.
- **Broker referral commissions** — when users open brokerage accounts (COL, BDO Securities, etc.) through Investagrams, they earn a referral fee
- **Virtual trading competitions** — entry fees + sponsor-funded prize pools
- **Educational content** — courses, webinars, bootcamps sold directly
- **Display advertising** — financial brand ads within the platform

**What this tells us:** Filipino fintech users WILL pay for premium features if the value is clear. Investagrams charges for advanced tools and earns. This directly validates our ₱149/month premium tier. The formula works in PH — premium subscription + affiliate commissions = sustainable fintech revenue.

---

### 8. GInvest / GCash — Ecosystem Monetization

- **Fund management fees** — GInvest earns a spread on UITFs placed through the platform. Typical fund expense ratios are 0.5–2% annually on AUM.
- **Transaction fees** — small fees on investment purchases and redemptions
- **Primary value: User retention in GCash ecosystem** — GInvest keeps users spending more time in GCash, which monetizes through GCredit (lending), GInsure (insurance), GCash Padala (remittances), and payment fees

**Scale:** GCash has a valuation of over $5 billion. GInvest is a retention feature, not a standalone P&L line.

**What this tells us:** GInvest will never be a neutral comparator — it's designed to keep money inside the GCash ecosystem. We are explicitly the alternative: neutral, cross-platform, shows you if somewhere else pays better than GInvest.

---

### 9. [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph) — Government + Infrastructure Fees

- **Transaction fees** — small percentage on RTB purchases and redemptions charged to investors
- **Government mandate / subsidy** — the Bureau of Treasury funds the platform as a public financial inclusion initiative
- **Infrastructure fees** — UnionBank and PDAX earn backend settlement and custody fees from the Treasury

**Scale:** Low direct commercial revenue. This is a public infrastructure play — its "success" is measured in RTB subscription volume and financial inclusion metrics, not profit.

**What this tells us:** [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph) is a referral partner for us, not a competitor. We show RTB rates in our comparison table → link to [Bonds.ph](http://Bonds.ph) → earn an affiliate fee when users subscribe. They get new investors; we get a commission. Win-win.

## The Four Strategic Insights

**Insight 1: No savings comparison tool has succeeded with a subscription model.**

Every content competitor is ad/affiliate-funded. Investagrams has a subscription, but it serves active stock traders with high willingness to pay for real-time data — a fundamentally different user than a savings rate comparator. Savings comparison is a low-frequency use case (users check rates monthly, not daily), making subscriptions a poor fit. **Our strategy: free tool, monetize through affiliate referrals and bank partnerships — the model every successful financial comparison site globally has proven.**

**Insight 2: Moneymax has proven CPA affiliate works at scale in PH.**

They built a NASDAQ-listed company on PH financial product referrals. Our Stream 2 follows the exact same blueprint — just applied to savings products instead of loans.

**Insight 3: Nobody does sponsored placement for savings rates.**

Moneymax charges banks to appear prominently for credit card and loan results. Nobody does this for digital bank savings rate promotions. This is an untapped Stream 3 revenue opportunity — Maya or UNO will pay to appear at the top of our table when they're running a promo.

**Insight 4: Finnest proves the product need, not the business.**

They built something useful and got Play Store traction with zero marketing. But they have no revenue model. We enter the same product space with a clear four-stream business model from day one: affiliate referrals, sponsored bank placement, newsletter-driven distribution, and B2B API.
